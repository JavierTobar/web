An introduction to mining Ethereum

Published:
February 02, 2021

Disclaimer : You should always do your own research before diving into unknown territory.
First of all, you should all know that Ethereum will (soon) fully transition to PoS. We don’t know when. Maybe in 2 years. We don’t know what will happen when this happens. We don’t know if mining will still be profitable nor which coin will be the next holy grail for miners. I decided to get into this because I’ve estimated that I would hit my ROI well before this would happen.

Nvidia Vs AMD : Which one should I pick?
TLDR : Pick Nvidia
  1. More algorithms
  2. Easier to stabilize
  3. Better resell value
Nvidia cards offer more mining algorithms, we don’t know what will happen after ethereum fully transitions to PoS, however Nvidia seems to be the more future proof card as it will allow you to mine anything. It is worth noting that Nvidia is relatively more stable than AMD in the sense that achieving stability is easier. When I first started mining, I had x5 5700xt. I tried to stabilize them for almost 3 weeks. I even had developers from hiveOS helping me directly and we could never stabilize them despite trying different OC settings or hardware switches. We came to the conclusion that it wasn’t any of the mining hardware. However, what were the odds that all 5 cards were defective? Slim to none if you ask me. The issue was simply that AMD was too hard for me to stabilize. This could’ve been my cue that mining wasn’t meant for me, but I decided to keep going. I decided to sell my AMD cards and buy Nvidia cards. This was single handedly the best decision I made. Nvidia cards are very stable and they’ve been running for weeks with no issues. Furthermore, the new ampere cards are wanted by gamers or other potential miners, this will increase their resell value. This is something to take into consideration because you need to have an exit plan.
If you're reselling your cards, please disclose that you've mined on them.
While most miners take great care in their cards, you should always be upfront to whoever you're selling your cards.

How do I get cards when everyone is using bots?
Unfortunately, you need to fight fire with fire. I wrote my own bot to auto buy cards for me, but I’m aware that not everybody is comfortable writing their own bot. At the time, I am not providing the source code for the bot because given the current situation, a lot of people are stealing code and selling bots on Facebook. You can use this bot instead of writing your own. The only difference is that you need to be on your computer because this bot will only open the webpage and checkout, but you need to purchase it yourself. Alternatively, you can join discord channels for notifications (this is probably the worst option, do this only if you’re not comfortable at all with running a bot).

Gold vs Platinum vs Titanium PSU
TLDR : It doesn’t really matter. Get Corsair or EVGA PSU.
This is an interesting topic. You can run your numbers and find out that spending an additional X amount of money on PSU A will save you Y amount of money in 6 years and is thus worth buying over PSU B. While this is true, you need to ask yourself : Am I really going to do this for 6 years? And after 6 years, you’re going to be saving ~10$/year? I don’t want to sound like a hypocrite since I’m all about the law of marginal gains, but 10$/year really is nothing. Unless you’re planning on build hundreds of rigs, you shouldn’t worry too much about this. With the ongoing pandemic there’s been a worldwide PSU shortage for the past 8 months as well. If you only have gold rated PSU available in your area, don’t wait for a restock on titanium PSUs, just get the gold PSU instead. I will say this however, titanium PSU tend to be better build quality than gold PSU but this really doesn’t matter. I would strongly recommend for you to get either Corsair or EVGA PSU. These manufacturers also offer a 10 year warranty on their PSUs. I personally like EVGA PSUs better because their cables are more manageable.
There’s another school of thought that revolves around server PSUs. I would not recommend these PSUs, they are extremely loud and very annoying to have in your household. If you’re planning on mining somewhere where the noise absolutely doesn’t matter, then go for it. You can find more information on PSUs on this website. The articles in there are very well written.
You need to think about how many GPUs you’re planning on having on a particular rig to determine what PSU you should get. e.g. if you want to plug 8 GPUs but your PSU only has x6 8 pin PCIE, you won’t be able to.

Which risers should I use?
Ok, before anything, let me tell you this. Don’t use sata connectors. Ever. Simply put, a GPU can draw up to 75 watts from its riser whereas a sata connector can only provide up to 54 watts. It's true that some cards don't draw the 75 watts from the riser, however the cards believe that they can draw 75 watts from the riser at any given time. What happens when you try passing 75 watts through SATA? Either the cable melts, the riser dies, the card dies, etc. You get the point.
My favourite risers are Ubit risers. They have a molex and PCIE connector. As well as an LED.
My second favourite risers are GPU Risers. They specialize in risers. They only offer a PCIE connector but there’s two different convenient locations for them, allowing you additional flexibility when building your rig. These risers are expensive but they do feel more premium. They have 2 bright LEDs.
My third favourite risers are mintcell molex risers. These have no LED and only offer a molex connection.
These 3 brands are very well known and respected in the mining community. Honestly, I wouldn’t recommend any other brand.

Which mining frame should I get?
It really doesn’t matter. You can even build your own. I built two of mine and bought one off ebay. Honestly, the one from Ebay is better than the two I built, but it did cost me 80$ since everything mining related is being scalped at the moment. Whatever you buy, just look at the price before the pandemic and ask yourself if you’re willing to overspend X amount. If it’s only 20$ more, go for it. If you haven’t built a rig before, I would recommend ordering one from Ebay/Amazon so that you can know how they’re meant to be built or features that are needed when you decide to build your own for your second rig. This is the one that I got. A lot of people will be very selective when buying frames saying “they’re flimsy” or “cheaply built”. It really doesn’t matter. It’s not like you’re gonna stand on it.

Which OS should I use?
HiveOS. That’s it, nothing more. The community is amazing and the software developers are greatly in touch with the community as well.

Which pool should I use?
Doesn’t really matter. You should, however, pick a pool that’s against EIP1559. Personally, I use nanopool. If you’re using hiveOS and have several rigs, Hiveon might be worthwhile. Another very popular pool is ethermine, 2miners and sparkpool. Personally, I haven’t heard of the other pools.

Which miner should I use?
T-rex miner. They specialize in Nvidia cards. The software developers are also nice people.
